Introduction

3D printed houses are built using large-scale 3D printers that extrude concrete or other building materials layer by layer to form the structure of a home. This method is known as additive manufacturing and is revolutionizing the construction industry by offering faster, cheaper, and more sustainable building options.

Global Projects

Wolf Ranch – Texas, USA

Developers: ICON & Lennar

Scale: 100 homes – the world’s largest 3D printed housing development

Features:

  • 2- and 3-bedroom models
  • Solar-powered
  • High resistance to wind and fire
  • Rounded concrete walls with a corduroy texture

Price: Starting just under $400,000

Impact: 75% of homes sold; a second, larger development is underway

BioHome3D – USA

Focus: Sustainability and affordability

Materials: Recyclable forest products and biodegradable materials

Innovation: Uses local wood supply and embedded sensors for monitoring

WASP – Italy

Philosophy: Eco-friendly, circular economy

Technology: Large-scale 3D printers capable of building homes in hours

Notable Projects: Homes and offices built with clay and natural materials

Citizen Robotics – USA

Specialty: Custom designs with near-zero construction waste

Strengths: Disaster resilience and sustainable practices

East 17th Street Residences – Austin, Texas

Developers: ICON & 3Strands

Significance: First 3D printed homes sold in the U.S.

Technology: ICON’s Vulcan system using Lavacrete, a cement-based material

Bosrijk, Eindhoven – Netherlands

Project: First multi-story 3D printed homes

Timeline: Construction began in early 2025

Size: 110–125 m² homes

Innovation: Marks a leap from single-story to multi-level 3D printed housing

Benefits of 3D Printed Concrete Homes

  • Speed: Homes can be printed in days
  • Cost: Lower labor and material costs
  • Sustainability: Reduced waste and carbon footprint
  • Durability: High resistance to natural disasters
  • Customization: Flexible design options

Ireland’s First 3D Printed Concrete Homes – Dundalk, Co. Louth

Location: Grange Close, Dundalk, County Louth

Homes: Three two-storey, three-bedroom terraced houses

Size: Each home has a gross internal area of 110 m²

Energy Rating: A2

Construction Time:

  • Printing: 12 print days
  • Total machine time: 18 working days
  • Overall build: 6 months (May–November 2024)

Technology: COBOD 3D construction printer on a gantry system

Construction Method: Fully printed cavity walls without concrete blocks

Key Partners

  • Harcourt Technologies Ltd (HTL) – Irish construction tech firm
  • Louth County Council
  • Roadstone – Materials supplier
  • Louth & Meath Education & Training Board (LMETB)
  • Harcourt Architects

Benefits & Innovations

  • Speed: 60% faster than traditional block construction
  • Efficiency: 35% reduction in total project delivery time
  • Cost: Approx. €253,000 per home (excluding VAT), comparable to traditional builds
  • Sustainability: Reduced waste and resource use
  • Social Impact: Provided homes for families on the local authority housing list
